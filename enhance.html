Why Brain Enhance?

Traditional contrast-enhanced MRI requires intravenous gadolinium — a heavy-metal agent linked to side-effects and contraindicated for many patients. Brain Enhance removes that barrier entirely.

Patient Safety First

Eliminates gadolinium exposure, removing risks of nephrogenic systemic fibrosis (NSF), allergic reactions, and heavy-metal deposition in the brain.

Faster Workflow

No IV line, no contrast injection wait-time, no post-injection monitoring. Reduce scan-to-report turnaround and increase patient throughput.

Broader Access

Enables contrast-equivalent imaging for patients with kidney disease, allergies, or pregnancy who would otherwise be unable to receive gadolinium.

Cost Reduction

Cuts contrast agent procurement costs, shortens scan sessions, and reduces nursing overhead associated with IV administration.

Diagnostic Confidence

Generates high-fidelity synthetic contrast with validated structural similarity, empowering radiologists with reliable tumour visibility.

Seamless Integration

Works with existing MRI hardware and PACS infrastructure. Feed standard FLAIR, T1 and T2 sequences and receive enhanced output instantly.

How It Works

Brain Enhance uses a 3D convolutional neural network with attention mechanisms to learn the mapping between non-contrast sequences and contrast-enhanced MRI.

Acquire Sequences

Standard FLAIR, T1 and T2 MRI volumes are captured using existing scanners.

Normalise & Align

Contrast normalisation and spatial alignment prepare the 3D volume for the network.

Neural Network Prediction

A 3D U-Net with attention analyses multi-sequence features to predict contrast enhancement patterns.

Tumour Enhancement

A dedicated contrast-enhancement block highlights tumour regions with precision.

Enhanced Output

A sharpened, contrast-enhanced volume ready for radiologist review — no gadolinium required.

Clinical Applications

Brain Enhance supports radiologists across a range of critical diagnostic scenarios.

Tumour Detection & Monitoring

Visualise enhancing lesions and track tumour progression over time without repeated gadolinium exposure, especially important for longitudinal follow-up.

Renal-Impaired Patients

Provide contrast-equivalent imaging for patients with chronic kidney disease or renal failure, who are otherwise excluded from gadolinium-based studies.

Paediatric & Pregnancy Imaging

Offer a safer contrast alternative for vulnerable patient populations where minimising chemical exposure is a clinical priority.
